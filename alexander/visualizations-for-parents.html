Visualizations for Parents

Alexander Alber · Interactive tools · 2026 · in German

A set of interactive visualizations built with Claude around the topic of pregnancy and early childhood. The tools are in German and use publicly available clinical data.

Pregnancy Timeline

Symptom timeline across pregnancy weeks

Maps 28 pregnancy symptoms as frequency-weighted bands across all 40 weeks, including trimester markers, a configurable "today" marker, and risk curves for miscarriage probability and premature birth survival. Data from NICHD 2013–18, Vermont Oxford Network, Magnus 2019, and Tong 2008.

Open tool →

Child Development Timeline

Developmental milestones and WHO growth curves, 0–48 months

Visualizes 49 developmental milestones across five domains (gross motor, fine motor, language, cognition, social-emotional) as lens-shaped bands showing typical onset, peak, and boundary. Overlaid with WHO percentile growth charts for weight and height, with optional input of current measurements. Sources: CDC/AAP 2022, WHO-MGRS, Michaelis et al.

Open tool →

Parental Leave Planner

Interactive planner for German parental leave benefit strategies

A month-by-month grid covering 24 life months, letting both parents independently assign Basis, Plus, or Partnerschaftsbonus months by clicking cells. Calculates total payout, joint months at home, and budget consumption in real time. Validates against current rules (April 2024 reform). Includes three preset models and slider inputs for net income.
